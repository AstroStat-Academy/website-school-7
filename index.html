Fear. No. Statistics.

Since 2019, we have been helping practitioners navigate the perilous waters of statistics and Machine Learning.

Are you an astronomer seeking to advance your research by delving deeper into statistics beyond the basics? Do you possess some familiarity with Machine Learning but are looking to demystify the intricacies of this "black box"?

The Summer School for Astrostatistics suits the needs of those who intend to "bridge the gap", and elevate their level from novice to mid-advanced. Each topic includes an introductory part along with hands-on analysis of astronomical data.

Technical RequirementsDue to its nature as a hands-on workshop, this event requires on-site attendance — we do not provide an option for remote participation. The attendees will need to bring their personal computers.

Topics

Five days of intensive, hands-on sessions covering the essential toolkit for data-driven astrophysics.

Classical & Bayesian Statistics

Foundational frameworks for hypothesis testing, parameter estimation, and inference.

AI Agents

Autonomous systems that plan, reason, and act — and how to harness them responsibly for scientific workflows.

Deep Learning

Neural network architectures for image recognition, signal classification, and generative modeling.

Gaussian Processes

Non-parametric regression for modeling uncertainty in time-series, light curves, and spectral data.

Markov Chain Monte Carlo

Sampling techniques for exploring complex posterior distributions in multi-parameter models.

Simulation Based Inference

Likelihood-free methods that leverage simulations to perform inference on intractable models.

Machine Learning Practices

Best practices for feature engineering, model validation, cross-validation, and reproducible pipelines.

Clustering, Classification & Regression

Unsupervised and supervised methods for discovering structure and extracting information from complex astronomical datasets.

GPU Parallelization

Accelerating computations with GPU programming for large-scale data analysis and simulations.

Teaching Body

Researchers and practitioners at the intersection of astrophysics, statistics, and machine learning.

Legacy

From a small workshop in Crete to an internationally recognized school. Six editions, five countries, one mission.

The AstroStatistics School was founded in 2019 by Jeff Andrews, Paolo Bonfini, Konstantinos Kovlakas, and Grigoris Maravelias at the University of Crete. What began as a grassroots initiative to bridge the gap between astronomy and modern statistical methods has grown into a sought-after international program.

Over five editions, the school has trained researchers from 21+ countries and 55+ institutes, building a growing community of data-literate astrophysicists. The 2026 Rome edition marks the school's first move beyond Crete, opening a new chapter under the DotCampus ecosystem.
